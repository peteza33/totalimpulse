Delta-V Required to Drop Altitude

{% endblock %} {% block content %}

This calculator compares the delta-V required to drop a spacecraft's orbital altitude while maintaining sun synchronicity. The major assumptions are

  • Circular orbit
  • Altitude drop maintains sun synchronicity (ie inclination is changed also)
  • First method is the summation of a Hohmann transfer plus a simple plane change
  • Second method is to combine the plane change with the tangential burn at apogee of the transfer orbit, which is more efficient

The calculator follows these major steps:

  1. The velocity of a circular orbit is defined as $$v = \sqrt{ \frac{\mu}{r_{earth} + alt}}$$ and a Hohmann transfer, the most fuel-efficient transfer between two circular coplanar orbits, is defined as $$dV_{Hohmann} = \bigg[ \sqrt{ \mu \Big( \dfrac{2}{r_{i}} - \dfrac{1}{a_{transfer}} \Big)} - v_{i} \bigg] + \bigg[ v_{f} - \sqrt{ \mu \Big( \dfrac{2}{r_{f}} - \dfrac{1}{a_{transfer}} \Big)} \bigg] $$
  2. The velocity change for a plane change is defined as $$dV_{incl} = 2 v_{i} \sin \theta $$
  3. The total 2-step process is then \(dV_{total} = dV_{Hohmann} + dV_{incl}\)
  4. The combined altitude drop and plane change velocity change is $$dV_{combined} = \sqrt{v_{i}^2 + v_{f}^2 - 2 v_{i} v_{f} \cos{\theta}}$$
